3.2.3. Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP)
In general, AHP is a mathematical method which is used
to evaluate EER neotectonic control on drainage basin sys-
tems and to derive their weights (Saaty, 1977; Saaty and
Vargas, 1980; Komac, 2006; Yao et al., 2008). In the pre-
sent study, we used AHP as a means of measurement which
deals with quantiﬁable and intangible criteria for the char-
acterization of regional tectonic activity. It is done in three
stages: i) to develop a matrix-based pair-wise comparison
of the eﬀect of each river morphometric parameters; ii) to
compute the weights for each factor at each level; and iii)
to estimate the consistency ratio (CR; Saaty, 1977).
1. For pair-wise comparison of the morphometric parame-
ters, thematic maps are generated and correspond to
speciﬁc weight values between 1 and 9 reﬂecting their
level of importance (Table 2; Saaty, 1977, Saaty and
Vargas, 1980). Once the pair-wise comparison matrix
is obtained, preferences are highlighted so that each fac-
tor can be assigned a proper relative importance. For
example, a score of 1 indicates equal inﬂuence of param-
eters and a score of 9 reﬂects the extreme inﬂuence of
one parameter over the others (Table 2).

criterion. The comparison of the degree of importance
