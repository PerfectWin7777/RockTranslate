Col 0 | Y=434 Complex geology and tectonics of the SW Cameroon Atlantic Coast are responsible for the present-day landscapes of the Edea – Eseka Region (EER). The EER is tectonically active and shows differential tectonic uplift, contrasting relief, variations in erosion rates, in river incision, and in channel gradient. Drainage system divides are dynamic features of a landscape that migrate over time during the development of river networks. To study the neotectonic variability in this region, we investigate the drainage river system and the topo graphic expression of active regional tectonics across the EER. From the Digital elevation model (DEM, 30 m pixel resolution) using Geographic Information System (GIS) interpretive techniques, we extracted eleven morphometric parameters of thirty-two main drai nage basins which were combined with Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) method. The inter-relationships between these indices grouped into four categories such as watershed geometry, relief characteristics, drainage texture analysis and regional tilting/uplift; can determine the influence of regional tectonic activity in the shape development of drainage basins. The results of the landscape anal ysis reveal significant variations of the morphometric parameters within the study area indicate a strong tectonic control. From the AHP values, the area is subdivided into high (  25 %), moderate (  43.75 %), and low (  31.25 %) regional tectonic classes. The spatial distri bution of different AHP classes shows a gradational pattern from coast to hinterland, suggesting a gradual decrease (from west to east) in the neotectonic activity in the EER drainage basin systems. Col 1 | Y=664 The Southern Cameroon margin includes the West Cameroon High-Plateau, South Cameroon Low Plateau and the Coastal Plain ( Fig. 1 b; Gèze, 1943; Sé galen, 1967; Olivry, 1986 ). The South Cameroon Low Plateau dis plays an eroded landscape dominated by alternating hills and inselbergs above the Precambrian basement. The Coastal Plain represents the lowest level of the Central African Surface with a few residual hills ( Boulvert, 1996 ). A dendritic hydrographic pattern with angular and parallel tendencies referring to a structural control is equally observed ( Owona et al., 2012; Nsangou Ngapna et al., 2013, 2018, 2019, 2020, 2023; Balla Ateba et al., 2021, 2023 ). EER belongs to Cameroonian Low Plateau where erased, residual, steep to high reliefs are built on Archean Congo shield, Proterozoic Nyong and Oubanguide com plexes and; Cretaceous Douala/Kribi-Campo sedimentary cover. EER residual surfaces exhibit a complex stepped landscape differentiated into three major geomorphic units, the coastal, intermediary and upper provinces; developed for first one on Douala/Kribi-Campo sedimentary basin and for two others, Nyong and Oubanguide complexes ( Nsangou Ngapna et al., 2013, 2019, 2020 ). Coastal pro vince or coastal plain geomorphic unit consists of deeply incised by the drainage patterns with few residual hills. Intermediary province includes middle- to high- residual reliefs whilst upper and hinterland geomorphic unit shows high reliefs moderated erased. EER landscape and drai nage patterns exhibit the dominant control of tectonics, lithology and climate of its particular morphology, in respect of CVL, CCSZ, SF and Benue Trough active regio nal features ( Nsangou Ngapna et al., 2013, 2018, 2019, 2020 ). Coastal (CP), intermediate (IP) and upper (UP) morphometric units demonstrated by Nsangou Ngapna et al. (2019, 2020) are considered here.

Col 2 | Y=604 6. Conclusion
Col 2 | Y=581 The Edea-Eseka region is a part of the Central-African surface and displays a complex morphological shapes with coexistence of three morphotectonic units (upper-, intermediate-, and coastal provinces) which evolved in the same geoclimatic conditions. Based on the qualitative and quantitative approaches, the morphotectonic analysis of the EER are implemented through eleven classical mor phometric parameters. These geomorphometric indices extracted from the Digital Elevation Model have been inte grated with the Analytical Hierarchy Process model in the aim to produce a reliable AHP map of the EER. According to the results, the spatial distribution of AHP shows high and moderate active zones. The first indicates a strong neo tectonic activity at  44.44 % in CP, 21.43 % in IP and   11.11 % in UP; and second displays a fairly moderate neotectonic activity at  33.33 % in CP, 57.14 % in IP  and  33.33 % in UP. Its appears at the end that the EER drainage basin systems shows moderate to high neo tectonic activity from the hinterland to coast at  68.75 %, which is probably due to the presence of two both regional (SF, DF and KF) and local (MeF, NgF and LoF) major fault systems. These faults may reactivate and can produce seismic activity in this study zone as well as in the South west Cameroon Atlantic coast. They also pinpoint the main role of the active CVL and particularly that of the Mount Cameroon volcano; all supported by lithospheric mantle dynamism/asthenospheric upwelling. This research has some reference value for EER drainage basins and that could eventually be applicable elsewhere.
